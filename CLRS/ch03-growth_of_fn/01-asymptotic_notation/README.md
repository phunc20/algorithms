




### little $o$
> _We use $o$-notation to denote an upper bound that is **not asymtotically tight**._

Put in another way, this is saying
$$
\begin{align}
  o(g(n))      & \subseteq O(g(n)) \\
  \Omega(g(n)) & \subseteq O(g(n)) \\
  o(g(n))      & \cap \Omega(g(n)) = \emptyset \\
\end{align}
$$
